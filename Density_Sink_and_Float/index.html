Three objects A, B, and C are placed in a liquid, as shown. Arrange them from the lowest density to the highest.

C < A < B

A < B < C

Solution

  • The object that completely sinks has more density than the water. So, C has the highest density among the three objects.

  • A and B are floating, so their density is lower than water. Among A and B, a larger fraction of A is immersed in water when compared to B. So, A has a higher density than B.

  • So, arranging the bodies in the decreasing order of densities, we get the following order : B < A < C

Amazing! You have successfully learnt if the density of an object is less than the density of a liquid, then it floats on the liquid, and if its density is greater than the density of the liquid, it sinks.
