At room temperature, particles of which of the following states of matter have the maximum movement speed?

Solution

• Solids and liquids have more closely packed particles as compared to gases.

• They are harder to compress as compared to gases because of strong forces of attraction between their particles.

•  Gas particles do not have these strong force of attraction between them and hence are free to move around and vibrate at very high speeds.

• Thus at a given temperature, gas particles show the maximum movement speed when compared to solid and liquid.

Wow! You have successfully learnt that gas particles have the maximum speed due to the minimum force of attraction between them.
