Solution

• Gas particles have almost negligible force    of attraction between them and hence    are free to move around and vibrate at    very high speeds.

• Since kinetic energy of the particles is    dependent on the temperature, so when    the temperature is decreased, the kinetic    energy decreases too.

•  Due to the decrease in kinetic energy of    the particles, their vibration and speed of    movement also decreases.

You just explored that on decreasing the temperature, kinetic energy decreases, which decreases the movement and vibration of the particles.
