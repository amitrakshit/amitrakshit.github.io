Step 1: Write the unbalanced chemical equation.
Al + O2 → Al2O3

Step 2: Make a list of the number of atoms of each element on reactant and product side.






Step 3: Pick an element and find out its least common multiple (LCM). Let us pick oxygen. LCM for 2 and 3 is 6.









Step 4: Divide the LCM with the number of atoms of that element and put the result as the coefficient on opposite sides.

Step 4a: There are 2 oxygen atoms on the reactant side; 6 divided by 2 is 3, so put 3 as the coefficient of oxygen on the reactant side.
Al + 3O2 → Al2O3





Step 4b: There are 3 oxygen atoms on the product side; 6 divided by 3 is 2, so put 2 as the coefficient of oxygen on the reactant side.
Al + 3O2 → 2Al2O3





Oxygen is balanced!

Step 5: Repeat Step 3 and 4 for aluminium.
4Al + 3O2 → 2Al2O3





Step 6: Balanced chemical equation.

Both reactant and product sides have equal number of atoms for each element. The chemical equation is now balanced!
